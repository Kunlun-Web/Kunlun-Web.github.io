    BeatProfiler

    Developed a multi-purpose scientific graphical user interface (GUI) for use in the field of cardiac imaging. The GUI fully automates the high-throughput pre-processing and analysis of cardiac fluorescence and brightfield imaging videos for monolayers and single cells of iPSC-CM and engineered heart tissues.

    Enabled precise, automated extraction of photobleach corrected traces, traditional feature metrics, single beat segmentation traces, and representations in spatial and frequency domain. The GUI supports over 5 video formats and includes advanced masking, photobleach correction, and signal peak algorithms for ultra-precise signal detection and segmentation.

    The GUI is designed to be easy to use, with a download-and-ready-to-use approach and drag-and-drop functionality for video input, allowing users to generate outputs without the need to write any code or install additional packages.

    Biomaterial & Drug delivery

    One of my biggest academic interests are novel biomaterial and drug delivery. I am most interested in 2 classes of drug delivery: lipoprotein-based drug delivery and synthetic-biology-based drug delivery. I first came into contact with lipoprotein as a potential drug delivery vehicle when I was taking Biochem645 (Molecular Control of Metabolism and Metabolic Disease). In class, I learned a lot about the mechanism of how lipoproteins transport cholesterol and triglycerides through a highly organized ligand-receptor recognition system, and this inspired me to think about its application in drug delivery. From my perspective, lipoproteins are like godsent gifts to cancer therapy research as they are biogenic, non-immunogenic, and intrinsically stable. Take LDL and HDL as an example, these magical cholesterol-delivering vehicles interact with cell types involved in lipid metabolism by binding to receptors such as SR-B1. And receptors like SR-B1 are found to be overexpressed in cancer cells, particularly in prostate and breast cancer cells, making LDL and HDL promising candidates to accurately target cancer cells.

    Nanorobots such as engineered platelets & RBC also attract me greatly because of their therapeutic delivery abilities. Such nanorobots are biocompatible and biodegradable and can be transfused into human body and used for targeting once ‚Äúinaccessible‚Äù body regions such as the brain. For my career goal, I hope to conduct research in cancer and drug delivery. I hope I will be able to solve the mystery of cancer by using information technologies such as RNA-seq and design an efficient drug delivery system for personalized therapies for cancer treatments.

    Functional genomics project

    This project used a single-cell transcriptomic dataset to analyze the transcriptional responses of A549 lung adenocarcinoma cells exposed to different anticancer therapeutics.

    The histone deacetylase inhibitors (HDACi) group had the highest degree of differential gene expression and Panobinostat was found to be one of the most effective treatments. PVT1 and CCND1 were identified as two genes that were significantly downregulated by Panobinostat. A co-regulation analysis was performed to investigate the relationship between PVT1 and CCND1 and revealed that they were part of a common pathway governed by the HDACi group.

    Further analysis of the co-regulation in modules 6 and 9, which contained PVT1 and CCND1 respectively, showed that the genes in these modules were downregulated by HDACi compounds. Reactome pathway analysis confirmed the relatedness between the genes in the modules and PVT1 or CCND1. The study suggests that Panobinostat may be effective in inhibiting the cell cycle-related tumorigenesis pathway through the downregulation of PVT1 and CCND1.

    Monocle3 Single-cell transcriptomic dataset Quality control Dataset exploration Differential expression analysis Gene set analysis Dimensionality reduction and visualization Co-regulation analysis Trajectory analysis

    ML classification of GBM subtypes

    Glioblastoma (GBM) is an incurable, poorly understood, aggressive type of cancer that occurs in the brain or spinal cord. Past studies have shown distinct gene expression between adult and pediatric GBM and their heterogeneity among patient populations.

    In this project, I developed a robust machine learning pipeline consisting traditional machine learning techniques and deep learning to classify adult and pediatric glioblastoma in a GBM single cell RNA sequencing dataset.

    The machine learning pipeline achieved a 99% precision, recall, and f1-score across 2265 testing samples and discovered 21 hallmark genes that could classify the two subpopulations at an average of 95% accuracy.

    Single cell RNA sequencing Machine learning Quality control Dimensionality reduction Clustering Marker genes identification Differential expression analysis Gene expression analysis
